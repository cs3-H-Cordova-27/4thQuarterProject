How is Biochemistry Important

Biochemistry is crucial because it explains biological processes, such as enzyme function and DNA/RNA mechanisms, which are foundational to understanding life. It advances medicine by aiding in disease diagnosis, drug development, and understanding disease mechanisms. In agriculture, biochemistry improves crop resistance and nutrition, contributing to better food security. It drives biotechnology, enabling genetic engineering and industrial applications like biofuels and enzymes. Additionally, biochemistry supports environmental science through bioremediation and maintaining ecosystem health.
