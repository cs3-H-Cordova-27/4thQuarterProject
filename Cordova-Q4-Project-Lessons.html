Introduction to Biochemistry

  • Definition and Scope

    • - What is biochemistry and why it is important.

  • Interdisciplinary Nature

    • - How biochemistry integrates biology and chemistry.

Biochemistry is the study of chemical processes within living organisms. It bridges biology and chemistry, focusing on the molecules that constitute cells and tissues. Key milestones in the field include the discovery of DNA's structure and the development of techniques like chromatography and electrophoresis.

LESSON 1

SUMMARY:

Proteins are large, complex molecules made up of amino acids. They play a crucial role in nearly every biological process, including catalysis (enzymes), structure (collagen), transport (hemoglobin), and defense (antibodies). Understanding protein structure (primary to quaternary) is essential for grasping their diverse functions.
